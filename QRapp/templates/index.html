Welcome to the State Museum and Zoo - Thrissur

The Diwan Peshkar of Cochin Province, Mr. Sankarayya, was the visionary behind the establishment of Viyyoor Park in 1885, located where the Central Prison stands today. Due to a lack of visitors, the park was closed in 1898. In 1912, the park was relocated and renamed the Cochin State Museum by acting Diwan K. Narayana Marar. Initially annexed to Krishna Vilasam Palace in Ernakulam, it later moved to Chembukkavu, where it resides today. In 1913, L.K. Anantha Krishna Ayyar was appointed as the museum's first full-time archaeologist.

The zoo was founded in 1914 by the King of Cochin, who generously donated animals from the Thrippunithura Kanakakkunnu Palace. Today, the State Museum and Zoo functions under the Department of Museums and Zoos, Government of Kerala. Spanning 13.5 acres, the zoo houses over 50 species of animals, including mammals, birds, and reptiles. The expansive compound also features an art museum, a multi-purpose museum, a botanical garden, a star garden, and a 3D theatre. Beyond being a center for recreation and education, the State Museum and Zoo play a crucial role in wildlife conservation and the preservation of cultural heritage in Kerala.

The current Art Museum building is located within the compound of the Thrissur Zoo. This century-old, majestic structure was once the residence of the famous educationist Shri I.N. Menon. The building was constructed using iron beams and Italian floor tiles. Notable exhibits in the museum include metal sculptures over 600 years old, a peacock lamp, Garuda lamp, port lamp, artwork of "The Last Supper" in medium straw on black cloth, Indonesian paintings, and much more.
